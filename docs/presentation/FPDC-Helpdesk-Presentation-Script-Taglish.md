# FPDC Helpdesk Ticketing System
## Taglish Presenter Script for Managers and Supervisors

**Audience:** Managers and Supervisors  
**Suggested delivery:** 45–60 minutes, plus demo and Q&A  
**Main message:** The Helpdesk is not only an IT tool. It is a standard process for accountability, visibility, prioritization, and better service.

---

## Slide 1 — FPDC Helpdesk Ticketing System

**Script**

“Good morning po, Managers and Supervisors. Today, ipapakita natin ang bagong FPDC Helpdesk Ticketing System.

Hindi lang po ito replacement ng chat, text, o verbal request. Ang goal natin is magkaroon ng isang formal at transparent process para sa lahat ng IT concerns—may record, may owner, may priority, at may status.

Mahalaga ang role ninyo dito because kayo ang tutulong mag-set ng expectation at mag-encourage sa teams na gamitin ang tamang process.”

**Transition:** “Let’s start with what we will cover today.”

---

## Slide 2 — Agenda

**Script**

“Dadaanan natin kung bakit kailangan ang system, ano ang benefits nito, at paano gamitin—from login hanggang ticket creation, tracking, conversation, at feedback.

May live demonstration din tayo. Ang objective is hindi lang makita ninyo ang features, kundi maintindihan kung paano ninyo ito ipapatupad sa inyong departments.”

**Presenter note:** Do not read every time block. Highlight the live demo, priority selection, and manager adoption.

---

## Slide 3 — Learning Objectives

**Script**

“After this session, expected na kaya nating gumawa ng complete ticket, pumili ng correct category at priority, mag-track ng status, at makipag-coordinate sa IT inside the ticket.

For managers and supervisors, may additional objective tayo: maging consistent ang inyong team sa paggamit ng system. Kapag may IT concern, ticket muna—hindi personal message, verbal request, o informal follow-up.”

**Key line:** “The system will only give us useful data if we consistently use it.”

---

## Slide 4 — Bakit Kailangan Natin Ito?

**Script**

“Sa old process, puwedeng dumaan ang request sa Viber, Messenger, text, tawag, o personal na sabi. Ang problema: mahirap malaman kung kailan nireport, sino ang may hawak, ano ang priority, at kung resolved na ba.

Sa Helpdesk, bawat concern may ticket number, audit trail, assigned IT staff, status, at complete history.

For management, this means less dependency on memory and follow-ups. May objective record tayo na puwedeng gamitin for monitoring, planning, at service improvement.”

**Ask the room:** “May experience po ba kayo na may request na nasabi na raw sa IT pero walang clear status o record?”

---

## Slide 5 — Ano ang Helpdesk System?

**Script**

“Ito ang central platform para sa hardware, software, network, email, security, at iba pang IT concerns.

Bawat request may unique ticket number. Makikita ang real-time status, may sariling conversation thread, may notifications, at may feedback pagkatapos ma-resolve.

Important point: ang buong communication tungkol sa issue ay dapat manatili sa ticket. Para kahit magpalit ang assigned staff o kailangan balikan ang history, kumpleto ang context.”

---

## Slide 6 — Benefits Para sa Inyo

**Script**

“For managers and supervisors, ang biggest benefits are visibility and accountability.

Makikita kung alin ang open, in progress, resolved, at closed. Hindi na kailangan magtanong sa iba’t ibang channels para lang malaman ang status.

Over time, makakatulong din ang records para makita ang recurring problems—for example, repeated printer issues, network interruptions, o common software concerns. Mas magiging data-driven ang IT planning at management decisions.”

**Key line:** “From individual complaints, makakabuo tayo ng organizational data.”

---

## Slide 7 — User Roles

**Script**

“May tatlong main roles. Ang User ay lahat ng FPDC employees—sila ang gagawa at magta-track ng tickets. Ang Admin ay IT staff—sila ang nag-a-assign, nag-u-update ng status, at nagma-manage ng users.

Ang Manager role ay para sa management visibility—analytics, reports, at feedback trends.

As managers and supervisors, puwede pa rin kayong gumawa ng sariling ticket. Pero leadership responsibility ninyo na siguraduhing ang employees ay sila mismo ang gumagawa ng ticket para accurate ang requester, department, at communication history.”

---

## Slide 8 — Step 1: Access the System

**Script**

“Pumunta sa helpdeskticketingsystem.vercel.app. Existing users can sign in; new users need to register using their own company details.

Please avoid shared accounts. One account per employee para tama ang ownership at audit trail ng bawat request.

As supervisors, magandang i-confirm after the session na registered at nakakapag-login ang members ng inyong team.”

**Demo cue:** Show login and registration briefly.

---

## Slide 9 — Step 2: Your Dashboard

**Script**

“Pag-login, makikita ang Overview, All or My Tickets, at Create Ticket.

The dashboard gives a quick view of ticket counts and status. Dito makikita ng user kung may open request, may ginagawa na ang IT, o resolved na.

Ang expectation natin: bago mag-follow up personally, i-check muna ang dashboard at ticket conversation.”

---

## Slide 10 — Step 3: Create a Ticket

**Script**

“Sa paggawa ng ticket, kailangan clear ang title, detailed ang description, at correct ang category at priority.

Ilagay kung kailan nagsimula, ano ang error, sino o ilang tao ang affected, at ano na ang troubleshooting na ginawa. Kung visible ang error, attach a screenshot.

One issue, one ticket. Kapag magkaibang concern, gumawa ng separate tickets para ma-assign at ma-track nang tama.”

**Demo cue:** Point out the screenshot attachment area and submit button.

---

## Slide 11 — Priority Levels

**Script**

“Ang priority ay based sa impact to work—not kung gaano natin gustong mauna.

Low means minor at kaya pang hintayin. Medium is a standard issue na kailangan ng attention within normal business operations. High means malaking epekto sa productivity. Critical means urgent at may immediate or widespread operational impact.

Gamitin natin ang quick check sa baba: *Sino ang affected, at kaya pa bang magtrabaho?*

Kung isang user lang at may workaround o kaya pang magpatuloy, usually Low or Medium. Kung maraming affected o tumigil ang important operation, maaaring High or Critical.

IT may validate or adjust the priority based on total business impact. Hindi lahat ng urgent para sa isang tao ay Critical para sa organization.”

**Example questions**

- “One employee cannot print but may another printer—Critical ba?”  
  **Answer:** Usually Low or Medium.
- “Whole office has no internet and operations stopped?”  
  **Answer:** Critical.
- “One department cannot send email and work is significantly affected?”  
  **Answer:** High; Critical only if the impact becomes widespread or operations stop.

**Key line:** “Kapag lahat Critical, wala nang tunay na Critical.”

---

## Slide 12 — Good vs Bad Ticket Example

**Script**

“Compare natin ang dalawang examples. Ang ‘Problem—ayaw gumana’ ay kulang. Mapipilitan pa ang IT na magtanong bago makapagsimula.

Ang good ticket may specific title, time started, exact error, device or user details, steps already tried, correct category, priority, at screenshot.

As supervisors, encourage your team to provide complete information. Better ticket quality means faster diagnosis and fewer back-and-forth messages.”

---

## Slide 13 — Step 4: Track Your Tickets

**Script**

“After submission, may ticket number at status: Open, In Progress, Resolved, then Closed.

Open means received. In Progress means may active work or investigation. Resolved means may solution na, while Closed means completed and archived.

Please avoid creating duplicate tickets just to get attention. If may update or follow-up, use the existing ticket conversation.”

**Management point:** “Status visibility reduces unnecessary escalation—but genuine business-impact changes should still be added to the ticket.”

---

## Slide 14 — Step 5: Ticket Conversation

**Script**

“Bawat ticket ay may sariling conversation between the employee and IT Support.

Dito ilalagay ang follow-up questions, additional details, screenshots, at updates. Naka-record ang sender, time, at full message history.

Please remind your teams: huwag ilipat sa personal Viber o Messenger ang discussion. Kapag nasa ticket ang conversation, complete ang context at puwedeng balikan ng employee, IT, at authorized management.”

**Key line:** “One concern, one ticket, one conversation thread.”

---

## Slide 15 — Notifications & Feedback

**Script**

“Users receive notifications for new messages, status changes, assignment, at resolution.

Kapag resolved na, may feedback option. Hindi ito formality lang. Ratings and comments help IT identify what works and where service needs improvement.

Managers can encourage honest and constructive feedback—specific, professional, at focused sa service experience.”

---

## Slide 16 — Best Practices: DO’s

**Script**

“Ito ang behavior na gusto nating maging standard: ticket for every IT concern, complete description, screenshot when useful, correct category and priority, at reply inside the ticket.

Important din ang one ticket per issue at regular checking of notifications.

As leaders, consistency starts with us. Kapag tayo mismo ay nag-personal message instead of creating a ticket, iyon din ang gagayahin ng team.”

---

## Slide 17 — Common Mistakes: DON’Ts

**Script**

“Iwasan ang personal messages as the main request channel, blank descriptions, misuse of Critical, duplicate tickets, at ignoring IT follow-ups.

Huwag ding mag-share ng credentials. At kung kaya ng colleague na gumawa ng sariling ticket, turuan natin sila instead of creating it for them.

The goal is not to make support difficult. The goal is to preserve accurate ownership, priority, history, and accountability.”

---

## Slide 18 — Category Quick Reference

**Script**

“Category helps route the ticket correctly: Hardware, Software, Network, Email, Security, or Other.

For suspicious email, phishing, password compromise, or security concern, choose Security and report immediately. Huwag mag-click ng suspicious links habang naghihintay ng response.

Kung hindi sure, puwedeng Other—but describe the issue clearly so IT can classify it correctly.”

---

## Slide 19 — Hands-On Activity

**Script**

“Let’s apply what we discussed. The scenario is a printer showing ‘Paper Jam’ kahit walang nakikitang stuck paper.

Before submitting, identify: clear title, complete description, Hardware category, at appropriate priority.

Hindi ito automatically Critical. Kung may ibang working printer at isang user lang ang affected, likely Low or Medium. Kung buong department cannot print an urgent operational document and no workaround exists, maaaring High.”

**Facilitation option for managers:** Ask them to verbally propose the title, description, category, and priority before the live entry.

---

## Slide 20 — Q&A, Comments, Suggestions

**Script**

“Open na tayo for questions, comments, and suggestions.

Aside from system features, gusto ko ring marinig ang implementation concerns: Ano ang possible resistance ng teams? Anong current process ang kailangan nating i-transition? Anong support ang kailangan from IT?

For resolution time, tandaan na priority guides triage and response—it is not an automatic promise of exact resolution time. Complexity, dependencies, and availability can affect completion.”

**Questions to ask managers**

1. “Who will remind or monitor your team during the first week?”
2. “Ano ang informal IT requests na kailangan nang i-convert into tickets?”
3. “May department-specific concern ba na kailangan nating i-consider?”

---

## Slide 21 — Closing and Call to Action

**Script**

“To close, ang success ng Helpdesk is not measured only by whether the system is online. Success means ginagamit ito consistently at tama.

Our next steps are simple: register or log in, use the system for all new IT requests, convert active informal requests into tickets, and send feedback if may challenges.

Managers and supervisors, we need your support in reinforcing the process. Kapag may employee na lumapit with an IT issue, guide them to create a complete ticket. This gives everyone better visibility, accountability, and faster coordination.

Maraming salamat po. The IT Department is ready to support your teams during the transition.”

---

As managers and supervisors, your role is important because system adoption follows leadership behavior. If we consistently require tickets, we gain visibility, accountability, and reliable data for decisions.”
