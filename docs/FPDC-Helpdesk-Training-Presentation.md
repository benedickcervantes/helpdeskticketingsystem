## SLIDE 4 — Bakit Kailangan Natin Ito?

### Problema ng dating paraan

| Old Way ❌ | Helpdesk System ✅ |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Text/Viber/Messenger sa IT staff personally | Formal ticket na may ticket number |
| Walang record kung sino ang nag-request | May audit trail at history |
| Nawawala ang request sa chat | Naka-save at ma-track ang status |
| Hindi alam kung sino ang nag-aasikaso | May assigned IT staff |
| Walang priority — lahat "urgent" | May tamang priority level |
| Walang feedback loop | May rating at suggestions after resolution |

> **Company policy:** Ang support requests na ginawa sa informal channels (personal messaging, verbal lang) **ay maaaring hindi ma-prioritize** at **hindi official record**.

**Speaker notes:** Be diplomatic — don't blame employees. Explain that the old way worked when the team was small, but as the company grows, we need a proper system.

## SLIDE 5 — Ano ang FPDC Helpdesk Ticketing System?

**Isang online platform** kung saan:

- 📋 Nagre-report kayo ng IT problems (hardware, software, network, email, security)

**Accessible sa:** Desktop, laptop, tablet, at mobile browser  
**URL:** https://helpdeskticketingsystem.vercel.app/

**Speaker notes:** Show the homepage briefly. Point out the "Get Started" button and FPDC branding.

## SLIDE 6 — Benefits Para sa Inyo (Employees)

### 1. Mas mabilis at organized na support
- Hindi na kayo maghahanap kung sino sa IT ang available
- Automatic routing sa tamang IT staff

### 2. Transparency — alam ninyo ang status
- **Open** — natanggap na ang ticket
- **In Progress** — may nag-aasikaso na
- **Resolved** — naayos na ang issue
- **Closed** — tapos na at na-document

### 3. May record — hindi na "nawawala" ang request
- Pwede ninyong i-check anytime ang history ng tickets ninyo

### 4. Real-time updates
- Notification bell sa system kapag may bagong message o status change

### 5. Voice ninyo ang mahalaga
- Feedback form pagkatapos ma-resolve — makakatulong sa IT na mag-improve

### 6. Professional at secure
- Company email account, encrypted login, role-based access

**Speaker notes:** Ask rhetorical question: "Sino dito na naranasan na nawala ang request sa chat?" — builds engagement.

## SLIDE 10 — Step 3: Create a Ticket (Live Demo)

### Sundin ang steps na ito:

**1. Title** — Maikli pero malinaw  
   - ✅ Good: *"Printer sa CRG hindi nagpi-print"*
   - ❌ Bad: *"May problema"* o *"Help"*

**2. Description** — Detalyado  
   - Ano ang nangyari?
   - Kailan nagsimula?
   - Ano ang error message (kung meron)?
   - Ano na ang sinubukan ninyo?

**3. Category** — Piliin ang tamang uri:

| Category | Halimbawa |
|----------|-----------|
| 🖥️ Hardware | Computer, printer, monitor, keyboard |
| 💻 Software | Apps, programs, system errors |
| 🌐 Network | WiFi, internet, connectivity |
| 📧 Email | Outlook, email account issues |
| 🔒 Security | Password, access, suspicious activity |
| ❓ Other | Iba pang IT concern |

**4. Priority** — Piliin nang tama (huwag i-overuse ang Critical):

| Priority | Kailan gamitin |
|----------|----------------|
| 🟢 Low | Minor, pwede hintayin |
| 🟡 Medium | Standard issue, kailangan sa business hours |
| 🟠 High | Malaking epekto sa trabaho |
| 🔴 Critical | Buong department/system down, urgent |

**5. Attachments (optional)** — Max 3 files, 5MB each  
   - Screenshot ng error, photo ng device, etc.

**6. Click Submit** — Makakakuha kayo ng **Ticket Number**!

**Speaker notes:** Do a live demo creating a sample ticket. Show the success message with ticket number.

## SLIDE 11 — Sample Ticket: Good vs Bad

### ❌ Bad Example
```
Title: Problem
Description: Ayaw gumana
Category: Other
Priority: Critical
```

### ✅ Good Example
```
Title: Outlook hindi makapag-send ng email — Billing Group
Description:
- Simula kaninang 9 AM hindi makapag-send
- Error: "Cannot connect to server"
- Restart ko na ang Outlook, same error pa rin
- Affected: Maria Santos, Billing workstation PC-12
Category: Email
Priority: High
Attachment: screenshot ng error message
```

**Speaker notes:** Explain that good tickets get resolved faster because IT doesn't need to ask follow-up questions.

## SLIDE 13 — Step 5: Ticket Conversation (Messaging)

Kapag kailangan ng IT ng karagdagang info, o may update sila:

1. Click ang ticket sa list
2. Buksan ang **Conversation** panel
3. Type your reply at i-send
4. Pwede mag-attach ng **screenshot/image** (max 3, 5MB each)
5. Makikita ninyo kung **"Seen"** na ng IT ang message ninyo

### Tips sa messaging:
- ✅ Sumagot sa loob ng ticket — hindi sa personal chat
- ✅ Be specific sa follow-up replies
- ✅ Mag-attach ng screenshot kung visual ang issue

**Speaker notes:** Demo the conversation feature. Show read receipts if available.

## SLIDE 17 — Best Practices (DO's)

### ✅ GAWIN:

1. **Laging mag-ticket** para sa lahat ng IT concerns
2. **Magbigay ng complete details** sa description
3. **Mag-attach ng screenshot** kung may error message
4. **Piliin ang tamang category** para sa faster routing
5. **Gumamit ng tamang priority** — huwag i-abuse ang Critical
6. **Sumagot sa ticket conversation** — hindi sa Viber/Messenger
7. **Mag-feedback** pag na-resolve na
8. **I-check ang notifications** regularly
9. **I-protect ang password** — huwag i-share
10. **Isang ticket per issue** — huwag i-combine ang unrelated problems

**Speaker notes:** These are the most important slide for day-to-day use. Consider printing as a handout.

---

## SLIDE 18 — Common Mistakes (DON'Ts)

### ❌ HUWAG GAWIN:

1. ❌ Mag-message sa IT personally sa halip na mag-ticket
2. ❌ Mag-submit ng ticket na walang description
3. ❌ Gamitin ang Critical priority para sa minor issues
4. ❌ Mag-create ng duplicate tickets para sa same issue
5. ❌ I-ignore ang follow-up messages ng IT sa ticket
6. ❌ Mag-share ng login credentials
7. ❌ Mag-submit ng ticket para sa colleague without their knowledge (better: turuan sila mag-ticket)
8. ❌ Umasa lang sa verbal request — walang official record

**Speaker notes:** Share real (anonymized) examples if you have them from early rollout.
