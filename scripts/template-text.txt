1. Purpose

This manual establishes the official standards and policies for the use of corporate email within Federal Pioneer Dev&apos;t. Corp.. It supports the transition from personal email accounts to company-managed corporate email and ensures that employees, HR, and management follow one consistent standard before system implementation.

The objectives of this policy are to:

Establish professional communication standards across all departments

Protect company, client, and employee information

Improve coordination among Sales, Marketing, Operations, Finance, HR, and IT

Ensure consistency when communicating with clients, brokers, developers, suppliers, and partners

Reduce business risk related to data loss, impersonation, and unauthorized disclosure

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

All full-time, part-time, probationary, and contractual employees

Interns and trainees issued a corporate email account

Authorized agents, brokers, or consultants using company email

All devices and platforms used to access corporate email

This policy covers:

Individual employee email accounts

Department and functional email accounts (e.g., sales@, info@)

Shared mailboxes

Email signatures, templates, and communication standards

4. Policy Statement
Official Policy

Federal Pioneer Dev&apos;t. Corp. requires all official business communication to be conducted using corporate email accounts, except where a written exception is approved by Management and IT.

Corporate email is a company asset. The company reserves the right to monitor, audit, retain, and retrieve email communications in accordance with this policy, applicable laws, and legitimate business needs.

6. Corporate Email Standards

6.1 Professional Communication

All emails sent using a corporate email account must maintain a professional tone. Employees must use proper grammar, complete sentences, and respectful language.

6.2 Consistency

A consistent format across all departments makes communication easier to understand and strengthens the company image through standard greetings, signatures, and layouts.

6.3 Accuracy

Before sending, employees must verify property/project name, unit or lot details, pricing, payment terms, client data, attachments, and recipient lists.

6.4 Accountability

Every email sent from a corporate account is considered an official company record. Employees are accountable for the content they send and forward.

8. Account Management

Corporate email accounts are created by IT only upon HR request or Management approval.

New employees must complete orientation, sign the acknowledgment form, set up password and MFA, and configure their official signature.

Email accounts must be suspended or deactivated upon resignation, termination, security incident, or role change.

HR must notify IT in advance for planned exits and immediately for urgent cases.

9. Acceptable and Prohibited Use

Acceptable Use

Client inquiries and follow-ups

Property presentations, quotations, and reservations

Coordination with brokers, developers, and suppliers

Internal approvals, reports, and project updates

Official notices from HR, Finance, and Management

Prohibited Use

Sending confidential company information to personal or unauthorized accounts

Harassment, discrimination, spam, or unauthorized marketing

Sharing login credentials or impersonating another employee

Sending unapproved pricing, discounts, or contractual commitments

Using company email for illegal activity or unauthorized side business

10. Security Requirements

Use strong passwords and never share credentials with anyone, including IT staff.

Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) where required.

Lock devices when unattended and log out from shared computers.

Report phishing, suspicious links, and lost devices to IT immediately.

Verify sender identity before opening attachments and never send passwords via email.

11. Confidentiality and Data Privacy

Employees must protect client personal information, reservation and sales records, contracts, commission details, financial records, and employee data.

The company expects compliance with applicable data privacy laws, including the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Philippines), where applicable.

Share only necessary client information

Use BCC appropriately for mass communications

Confirm recipient authorization before sending contracts, IDs, or financial documents

12. Email Composition and Layout

Greeting — professional salutation such as &apos;Dear Mr. Santos,&apos; or &apos;Good day,&apos;

Introduction — state the purpose immediately

Main Message — provide details in a clear and organized manner

Action Required — indicate if a response, approval, payment, or confirmation is needed

Closing — end the email professionally

Signature — include the complete corporate signature

15. Email Usage by Function

Property Inquiries — respond to buyers, investors, tenants, and property seekers using corporate email.

Reservation and Sales Transactions — document quotations, payment instructions, and confirmations through corporate email.

Internal Communication — use email for approvals, reports, project updates, and coordination.

Customer Service — handle concerns, complaints, and follow-ups professionally.

HR and Administrative Communication — official announcements must come from authorized HR or Management accounts.

Once a corporate email account is issued, employees must not use personal email for official company transactions, client communication, or internal business records.

17. Migration from Personal to Corporate Email

The company will announce an official migration schedule and cutover date.

All new client communication must use corporate email after the cutover date.

Employees must notify active clients, brokers, and partners of their official corporate email address.

Employees must not continue sending official quotations or contracts from personal email after migration.

IT and HR will provide account setup, signature configuration, orientation, and support contacts.

18. Shared Mailboxes and Department Accounts

Access is granted only to authorized personnel approved by the Department Head and IT.

Shared mailboxes must be used only for their intended business purpose.

Employees must identify themselves when replying from a shared mailbox, if required.

Unresolved client issues must be escalated to the supervisor.

If a complete response is not yet available, send a brief acknowledgment stating that the request is being processed.

20. Email Retention and Archiving

Corporate email may be stored, archived, and retrieved for business, legal, audit, and security purposes.

Employees must not delete emails related to pending transactions, complaints, contracts, or legal matters without approval.

Upon separation, company access to business-related email records remains with the company.

21. Employee Turnover and Offboarding

Endorse active clients to the assigned replacement employee or supervisor.

Turn over important email threads related to reservations, contracts, payments, and approvals.

Return access to shared mailboxes and confirm no personal recovery information remains linked to company accounts.

Include all ongoing reservations, listings, contracts, and approvals in the turnover report.

IT will disable email access on the employee&apos;s last working day unless Management approves a limited extension.

22. Monitoring, Compliance, and Disciplinary Action

The company may monitor corporate email usage to protect systems and data, investigate security incidents, ensure policy compliance, and support audit and legal requirements.

Violations may result in verbal or written warning, temporary suspension of email access, disciplinary action up to termination, or legal action if applicable.

Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information

Use of personal email for official business after migration

Harassment or unprofessional communication

Sharing of credentials or fraudulent client communication

23. Training and Acknowledgment

Employees must attend orientation on email policy and basic security practices.

Employees must configure approved email signatures.

Employees must sign the Employee Acknowledgment Form.

HR will maintain records of signed acknowledgments.

No employee should be considered fully onboarded to corporate email until orientation and acknowledgment are completed.
